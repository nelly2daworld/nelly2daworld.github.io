Where is Guyana?

Guyana (GUY-ah-nah) not (GHANA) is located at the North-Eastern edge of South America bordered by Venezuala, Brazil and Suriname. Many people ask if English is my second language because I was born in South America; however, Guyana is the only English speaking country and only nation state of the Commonwealth of Nations in South America. Additionally, Guyana is the third-smallest country on the mainland of South America.

Fun Facts about Guyana

Guyana is a land of six peoples! Unsurprisingly, our national motto is "One People, One Nation, One Destiny". As can be seen from the image, the flag colors are green, red, yellow, white and black. There are ten administrative regions in Guyana.









Kaieteur Falls: World's largest single drop waterfall (741 ft) by the volume of water flowing through it.

An aerial view of a portion of Guyana's coastal area. Fun Fact: Ninety percent of Guyanese lives on the coastal strip (predominantly Region 4) and only accounts for ten percent of Guyana's land mass.
